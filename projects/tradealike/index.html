How it usually works

It is not a bad trade that evaporates your account, but the emotional attachment and lack of discipline that keeps you from closing that losing trade. We like to call this phase 'The Good, The Bad, The Ugly". Here is an example:

The Good

You make three profitable trades on the long side by getting in every time the stock retraces a little bit.

The Bad

When it retraces the fourth time, you open a long position again. But this time the stock keeps going down. However by now you are so biased towards keeping your long trade open because you think you cannot be wrong. You made money in the previous three trades the same way.

The Ugly

Now this last trade has already eaten more than half of your profit from the previous three trades. But now you feel compelled to hang on to it a little longer hoping that it would turn back up. We all know what happens after that.

What we trade

We trade Single-Leg option contracts, and Vertical Credit spreads.

Single-Leg Option trades

These are naked call and put trades seeking returns in 25% to 40% range. We incorporate trend and momentum based studies along with intra-day oscillators to find better entry points. The helps in closing the trade in green sooner versus later.

Vertical Credit spreads

High success probability vertical spreads to generate 5% to 10% income every week. We open a trade latest by Monday or Tuesday of the trading week that has more than 80% chance of expiring worthless so we get to keep the credit received. In case the trade is not working as anticipated, we can always close it before the options expire.

FAQ

What is it that you trade?
We trade single leg Call/Put option contracts, as well as Vertical Credit Spreads (both Bull Put & Bear Call).
How will I be notified of a trade?
Our members receive timely notifications on their iPhones with precise information on what to buy.
How many trades are made each week?
We do not have a particular rule that we stick to. It is the disciplined approach that dictates that schedule. However, on average we intend to trade 2 single leg trades, and at least 1 vertical spread.
Do you help manage and close the trade?
Yes, we do send out follow-up notes and trade close alerts.
What do I need to do to receive the weekly trade alerts?
Simply download our mobile app 'TradeAlike' via the iTunes store, and follow the instructions there. Downloading the app is 100% free.
How long is each position held?
There is not a set amount of time, but most of the trades are open from 2 to 5 days including the vertical spreads. The point is to read the chart and as long as the price action and technical indicators are intact, we keep the trade open.
Can I trade Vertical Spreads only?
Yes, you can select the trading strategy and set ups as per your liking, and market outlook. If you are Bullish, but a little conservative, you can sign up to only receive Bull-Put Vertical Credit spread.

What is the average contract price that you trade?
It all depends on the month of expiration, strike price, what kind of stock is it (High priced or not), etc.
How do I subscribe to your service?
Kindly sign up by downloading our iPhone app via iTunes . The name of the app is "TradeAlike".
When do you enter trades?
There is no exact time when to enter a trade, you need to make sure you get the best entry price possible. Also remember we trade on our own alerts as well and only trade during New York market hours which is normally between 9:30AM to 4PM EST.
What should I do if the asset price is above or below the entry price in the alert?
To play it safe, if by the time you place the trade the price is already worse than our alert price, we recommend you don't enter it or use the limit order to make sure that risk to reward ratio works in your favor. We have many traders that will take every alert, regardless of the price, and it works for them. But, we use our signals with limit orders.
